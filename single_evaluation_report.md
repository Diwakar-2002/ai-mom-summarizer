## SUMMARY
The Civil Rights Utilities, Economic Development and Arts Committee discussed Resolution 31669 which encourages landlords to use individualized tenant assessments based on the Fair Housing Act's discriminatory effect standards when considering criminal history in screenings. The resolution was amended by substituting D4 for D3, adding a recital about lifting up HUD guidance and referencing Seattle’s Fair Chance employment ordinance to ensure individualized assessments are conducted without bias towards applicants with prior arrests. It also includes principles from the HALA recommendations aimed at distinguishing between conduct indicating risk to resident safety versus non-risk behaviors, as well as clarifying expectations in line with HUD guidance for landlords' policies on criminal records.

The committee discussed an amendment proposed by Councilmember Bagshaw that recognizes the state legislation concerning certificates of restoration of opportunity (CROP), which provides concrete and objective information about individuals who have served time, to facilitate their reintegration into society with respectable housing and employment opportunities.

## ACTION ITEMS
- Council Rule 3A6 suspended for the presentation of amendments after noon deadline today (Speaker B).
- Amendment proposed by Councilmember Bagshaw to recognize certificates of restoration of opportunity added into the resolution, which received a second and was adopted.
